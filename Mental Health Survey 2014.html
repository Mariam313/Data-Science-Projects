Mental Health in Survey 2014

This dataset was produced by Open Sourcing Mental Health Illness (OSMI) in 2014. 1200 employee from the tech industry were asked 27 questions to measure the attitude towards mental health and the frequency of mental health disorders in the tech workplace.

In additon to demographic information, the following informatin were extracted:

  • Self-employed: Whether the respondent is self-employed (Yes or No).
  • Family history: Whether the respondent has a family history of mental health issues (Yes or No).
  • Treatment: Whether the respondent has sought treatment for a mental health condition.
  • Work interference: Whether mental health issues affect the respondent’s ability to perform work (Often, Sometimes, Rarely, or Never).
  • No. of employees: The size of the company the respondent works for, categorized by ranges like 1-5, 6-25, 26-100, etc.
  • Remote work: Whether the respondent works remotely (Yes, No, or Sometimes).
  • Tech company: Whether the respondent works in a technology company (Yes or No).
  • Benefits: Whether the company provides mental health benefits (Yes, No, or Don’t know).
  • Care options: Whether the respondent knows about the care options for mental health in their company.
  • Wellness program: Whether the company offers a wellness program (Yes, No, or Don’t know).
  • Seek help: Whether the respondent would feel comfortable asking for time off due to a mental health issue.
  • Anonymity: Whether the respondent believes anonymity is protected if they were to take advantage of mental health benefits.
  • Leave: Whether the respondent thinks taking a mental health leave would negatively impact their career.
  • Mental health consequences: Whether the respondent believes there are negative consequences at work for discussing mental health issues.
  • Physical health consequences: Whether the respondent believes there are negative consequences at work for discussing physical health issues.
  • Coworkers: Whether the respondent would discuss mental health issues with coworkers.
  • Supervisor: Whether the respondent would discuss mental health issues with their supervisor.
  • Mental health interview: Whether the respondent believes mental health would be a factor in hiring decisions.
  • Physical health interview: Whether the respondent believes physical health would be a factor in hiring decisions.
  • Mental vs. physical health: Whether the respondent feels their employer takes mental health as seriously as physical health.
  • Observed consequences: Whether the respondent has observed or heard of negative consequences for someone discussing mental health in the workplace.
  • Comments: Free-text responses where respondents can leave additional comments.
